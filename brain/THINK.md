# THINK — Structured Thinking Methods

> **Role**: 20 methods for when you need to think better, not harder.
> **Activation**: Say "use [method]" and the AI applies that method.

---

## When to Use THINK

Use THINK when the user asks for analysis, or when the AI needs to reason through something complex.

Do NOT reach for THINK when:
- The answer is straightforward (just answer)
- It's a factual lookup (just search)
- It's emotional support (just listen)

---

## The 20 Methods

### 🔍 Analytical — Understand what's really happening

**First Principles** 追问本质
> Strip all assumptions. Re-derive from basic constraints.
> Ask: "Why is this actually true?"

**Feynman Test** 说人话
> Can you explain this to someone with zero background?
> If not in 3 sentences without jargon, you don't understand it.

**Reverse Thinking** 逆向推演
> Start from the goal. Work backwards.
> "What needs to be true for this to work?"

**Reductionism** 微观拆解
> Break into the smallest verifiable sub-problems.
> "What is the simplest version that proves the concept?"

**Limit Observation** 极限观察
> Find 3+ distinct details that don't fit the pattern.
> "Look again. What did you miss the first time?"

---

### 🎨 Creative — Break out of the obvious

**Cross-Disciplinary** 跨界碰撞
> How does nature solve this? How does finance? Biology? Physics?
> "What can we import from an unrelated field?"

**Analogy Transfer** 异域映射
> What proven pattern from another domain applies here?
> "This is like X — what can we copy?"

**Extreme Hypothesis** 极端假设
> Remove one core constraint. What changes?
> "If time/money/safety were unlimited, what would we do?"

**Negation of Negation** 否定的否定
> Deny the current approach. Rebuild from scratch.
> "What if everything about this method is wrong?"

**Divergent Mapping** 脑暴联想
> Rapid-fire free association. No filter. Write everything.
> "What connects to this? And to that?"

---

### 🛡️ Verification — Check your work

**Devil's Advocate** 魔鬼代言
> Build the strongest counter-argument.
> "Here is why this could be completely wrong."

**Boundary Testing** 边界冲击
> Push to extremes. Where does it break?
> "10x larger? 10x smaller? Zero? Infinite?"

**Consistency Check** 自相矛盾
> Cross-reference all known facts. Any contradictions?
> "Does this conflict with anything we know is true?"

**Occam's Razor** 奥卡姆剃刀
> Given equal explanatory power, choose the simplest.
> "Is there a boring explanation that fits just as well?"

---

### 🧭 Guidance — Make better decisions

**Goal Anchoring** 目标锚定
> Does this action serve the core objective?
> "If I do this, does it move us toward the goal?"

**Opportunity Cost** 机会成本
> Every choice has a price. What are we giving up?
> "By choosing this, what are we NOT doing?"

**Second-Order Effects** 二阶效应
> Immediate → knock-on → long-term.
> "What happens after the immediate result?"

**Positive Guidance** 正向提炼
> Extract the repeatable pattern from success.
> "Why did this work? How do we replicate it?"

---

### ⚡ Speed — When time is short

**Rapid Pattern Scan** 速判扫描
> 30 seconds. Find the one most important thing.
> "What is the key insight? Go."

**Encoding Sprint** 编码冲刺
> Convert complex information into something memorable.
> "Story, image, number — what sticks?"

**Spatial Indexing** 空间索引
> Map information to physical locations.
> "If this information were in a room, where would it go?"

## Method Selection Guide

| Situation | Best Method | Secondary |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| "Why is it like this?" | First Principles | Reductionism |
| "How do I solve this?" | Reverse Thinking | Analogy Transfer |
| "Is this even possible?" | Limit Observation | Boundary Testing |
| "What's the right choice?" | Opportunity Cost | Second-Order |
| "How do I remember this?" | Encoding Sprint | Spatial Indexing |
| "Why did it succeed?" | Positive Guidance | — |
| "I need a breakthrough" | Divergent Mapping | Extreme Hypothesis |
| "Something feels wrong" | Devil's Advocate | Consistency Check |

---

## Speed Mode

When the user says "quick" or "quick mode":
> Give the shortest correct answer. No preamble. No explanation unless asked.

When the user says "think about this":
> Apply the most relevant method. State which method you're using and why.
