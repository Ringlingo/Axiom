# Axiom — References & Inspiration

Axiom is not built on novel research. It synthesizes established thinking from software engineering, cognitive science, and practical AI use.

---

## Core Intellectual Sources

**Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, Fast and Slow*** — Dual-process theory (System 1/System 2) informed the THINK module structure.

**Baddeley, A. D. (1974). Working Memory** — Inspired the FOCUS/CONTEXT distinction between persistent and working context.

**Dresler, M. et al. (2017). Mnemonic Training Resshapes Brain Networks** — Referenced for spatial indexing in THINK's Encoding Sprint method.

---

## Practical Inspiration

**Engineering Safety Culture** — The five AXIOM laws are modeled after the principles of defensive programming and aviation checklists.

**Software Project Conventions** — RULES.md is inspired by team on boarding documents and project READMEs.

**Operational Runbooks** — CONTEXT.md structure draws from incident logs and engineering decision records.

---

## Open Source References

- **Mem0** (github.com/mem0ai/mem0) — Memory layer concepts for AI agents
- **Letta** (github.com/letta-ai/letta) — Stateful agent architecture
- **Claude Code** (claude.com/code) — Practical cognitive layer design for AI coding

---

## Axiom Is Not

Axiom does not claim to simulate consciousness, model actual neurons, or replicate brain mechanics. It is a behavioral engineering framework, not a neuroscience claim.
